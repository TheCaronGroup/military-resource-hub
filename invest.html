Advanced Strategy

The Multi-Unit VA Loan Strategy

One of the most powerful — and least used — VA loan strategies available.

The VA loan can be used to purchase a duplex, triplex, or fourplex — as long as you live in one unit as your primary residence. You rent out the remaining units. In San Diego's rental market, a well-chosen multi-unit property can generate enough rental income to nearly cover your entire mortgage payment.

When you PCS, you convert your primary unit to a rental and the property becomes a cash-flowing investment. You exit San Diego owning a multi-unit income property — often with zero down payment. This is how military families build real long-term wealth through service.

What qualifies
2–4 unit residential properties where you occupy one unit as primary residence
Rental income credit
Lenders may count projected rental income toward qualification — ask your VA lender specifically
San Diego opportunity
Duplexes and triplexes exist in many military-adjacent neighborhoods at realistic price points
Talk to Ryan first
Multi-unit VA purchases require specific lender and property qualification — get guidance before you shop
Core Concepts

How Military Families
Build Wealth Through Real Estate

🏠
The PCS Rental Strategy
Buy a condo or townhome near your San Diego installation. Live in it while stationed here. When PCS orders come, evaluate converting it to a rental instead of selling. If the rental income covers the mortgage — or close to it — you own an appreciating asset that generates income while you are gone. This is how many military families build their first investment property without even planning to.
🏠 Property Management When You PCS

If you convert your home to a rental at PCS time, you will need to decide: self-manage or hire a property manager. For most military owners — especially those going overseas or to another coast — a property manager is worth the 8–10% management fee. They handle tenant screening, maintenance calls, lease enforcement, and local compliance. Ryan can refer you to trusted military-friendly property managers in San Diego.

Property management fee: typically 8–10% of monthly rent collected
🔄
Using Your VA Benefit Twice
After converting your first home to a rental and PCSing, many veterans can use their VA benefit again at their new duty station — either by restoring full entitlement after the first loan is paid off, or by using remaining entitlement for a second purchase. This creates the possibility of owning two properties with VA financing over a career.
📈
Equity and Appreciation
Every mortgage payment reduces your principal balance and builds equity. In San Diego, historical appreciation has generally trended positive over time — meaning the home typically grows in value. Combined with principal paydown, owning for 5+ years typically results in significant net worth growth even accounting for all costs.
💵
Cash Flow vs Appreciation
In San Diego, most first purchases are appreciation plays — the property may not cash flow positively from day one due to high prices. However, rent grows over time while your mortgage payment stays fixed. A property that barely breaks even at purchase often becomes cash-flow positive within 3–7 years. The long-term math works in your favor.

Important context: Real estate investing is not risk-free. Markets fluctuate, vacancies happen, and maintenance costs are real. This section is educational — not financial advice. Every situation is different. Talk to Ryan and consult a financial advisor before making investment decisions.

Educational Scenario

A Real-World Example
(Educational, Not a Guarantee)

Scenario: E-6 Buys a Condo Near Miramar, Then PCSs
📆
Year 1: Purchase
Buys a 2BR condo in Mira Mesa for $640K using VA loan, zero down. BAH covers ~$3,500 of the $4,100 total monthly cost (mortgage + tax + HOA + insurance). $600/month out of pocket.
📈
Years 1–4: Builds Equity and Appreciation
Each mortgage payment builds equity. If SD appreciates at a modest 3% annually, the home grows to approximately $720K by year 4. Combined with principal paydown, equity approaches $100K+.
📦
Year 4: PCS Orders
Receives PCS orders. Rents the condo for $2,800/month. Mortgage is $3,400. Out-of-pocket difference is $600/month — but the asset is growing, equity is building, and in 3–5 years the rent will likely cover or exceed the payment.
🎉
Year 10: The Picture
If appreciation continues at 3%/year, the home is worth ~$860K. Rental income has grown to ~$3,200+. The property is likely cash-flow neutral or positive. Net equity: $200K+. This is how a service member who started with zero down built substantial wealth through a single strategic purchase.
